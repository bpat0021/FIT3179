Cycling Data Visualisation

Who Cycles?

Australia is known for its' sunny weather and amazing cities. Does our population take advantage of this and go out and cycle?

The two visulisations on the left aim to show how many people cycle in Australia, normalised as a percentage of the population.
Both visualisations are categorically split, with the line graph split by gender and the bar chart split by state. Normalised values for the bar chart are by state populations.

From the graphs a downward trend in participation is apparent. It also shows that women tend to consistently cycle less and states such as SA are usually under the national average. This suggests a national level change is required to bring up rates for everyone.

Facilities for Cyclists

Let's take a closer look at Victoria. This state regularly performs close to the national average in terms of participation rate.

If you are a cyclist you will be happy to see the amount of bike parking facilities in the capital city. On the otherhand they are much sparser in a regional city like Ballarat. This doesn't follow participation rate trends shown in the Lollipop graph below.

The lollipop graph rather shows that regional victoria typically has higher participation rates. This may suggest that avaliability of facilities, although convinent aren't as impactful as potential other factors on how much a population cycles.

The Road Ahead...

As of 2019, Melbourne's blue bike scharing scheme was scrapped and eventually replaced by electric scooters. It is evident that among the newer generation there is increased adoption of these electric transport technologies.

As can be seen on the pie chart on the right, a majority of E-scooter users are emerging. Although push bikes are still the popular choice in the future, E-scooters are expected to overtake based on a report , which was also the source of the data for the piechart.

To prevent this and to lift up the already low usuage of bicycles in Australia, this visualisation calls upon the government to introduce policy. This is so Australian citizens are able to enjoy the benefits of cycling!
